Basic

When you select an indication, the calculator uses the corresponding dosing for amoxicillin-clavulanate from UpToDate. Based on the patient's weight, it then calculates the appropriate quantity of each formulation of amoxicillin-clavulanate to satisfy the amoxicillin dosing requirement. It then calculates the clavulanate that would be delivered by each formulation. The calculator only displays the formulations that have an appropriate amount of clavulanate and are able to deliver the necessary amount of amoxicillin ± 10%.

Intermediate

Dosing settings:

  • Age <3 months: 30 mg/kg/day (age-based dosing takes precedence over indication-based dosing below)
  • Impetigo: 25 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 875 mg/dose
  • Acute otitis media: 90 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 4,000 mg/day
  • Intra-abdominal infection: 45 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 875 mg/dose
  • Community-acquired pneumonia: 90 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 4,000 mg/day
  • Sinusitis: 45 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 875 mg/dose
  • Dog/cat bite: 45 mg/kg/day divided BID, max 875 mg/dose
  • Chronic Group A Strep carrier: 40 mg/kg/day divided TID, max 500 mg/dose and 2,000 mg/day
  • UTI: 30 mg/kg/day divided TID, max 500 mg/dose and 2,000 mg/day

Dosing calculation:

  1. With respect to the selected indication's amoxicillin dosing, calculate the ideal dose of each amoxicillin-clavulanate formulation
  2. Calculate quantity (mL or # tabs) to match the selected dosing
    • Using the below measurable dosing increments, round the calculated ideal quantity to avoid impractical doses like 1.29 tablets or 2.837 mL
      • Liquid: 0.1 mL if <3 mL, 0.2 mL if >3 mL
      • Tablet: 0.5 tablets
    • Round both up and down to display an acceptable range of doses
      • Example: if the ideal quantity is 1.736 tablets, round down to 1.5 tablets and round up to 2 tablets, yielding a range of 1.5–2 tablets
  3. Calculate amoxicillin dose in mg/dose based on the calculated practical quantities from Step 2
    • Example: 1.5–2 tablets of amoxicillin-clavulanate 500–125 tablets would yield 750–1000 mg amoxicillin per dose
  4. Using the selected dosing frequency, convert the amoxicillin dose to mg/day
  5. Calculate clavulanate dose in mg/day based on the calculated practical quantities from Step 2
  6. Flag the formulations in which either the rounded down or rounded up clavulanate dose is 6–10 mg/kg/day if <40 kg or 250–375 mg/day if >40 kg
    • If weight is >40 kg, hide the ES formulation regardless of clavulanate dose
    • If weight is <40 kg, hide the XR formulation regardless of clavulanate dose

Worked Examples

7-year-old (27 kg) being treated for community-acquired pneumonia

Community-acquired pneumonia: amoxicillin 90 mg/kg/day divided BID; clavulanate 6–10 mg/kg/day

Amoxicillin = 90 mg/kg/day × 27 kg = 2,430 mg/day | 2,430 mg/day divided BID = 2,430 mg/day ÷ 2 dose/day = 1,215 mg/dose

Clavulanate minimum = 6 mg/kg/day × 27 kg = 162 mg/day | 162 mg/day divided BID = 162 mg/day ÷ 2 dose/day = 81 mg/dose
Clavulanate maximum = 10 mg/kg/day × 27 kg = 270 mg/day | 270 mg/day divided BID = 270 mg/day ÷ 2 dose/day = 135 mg/dose

Amoxicillin:clavulanate ratio = 1,215:81–1,215:135 = 15:1–9:1; out of the available formulations (2:1, 4:1, 7:1, 14:1, 16:1), 14:1 is the only one that is appropriate

2-month-old (5 kg) being treated for recurrent acute otitis media

Age < 3 months (regardless of indication): amoxicillin 30 mg/kg/day divided BID; clavulanate 6–10 mg/kg/day

Amoxicillin = 30 mg/kg/day × 5 kg = 150 mg/day | 150 mg/day divided TID = 150 mg/day ÷ 3 dose/day = 50 mg/dose

Clavulanate minimum = 6 mg/kg/day × 5 kg = 30 mg/day | 30 mg/day divided TID = 30 mg/day ÷ 3 dose/day = 10 mg/dose
Clavulanate maximum = 10 mg/kg/day × 5 kg = 50 mg/day | 50 mg/day divided TID = 50 mg/day ÷ 3 dose/day = 17 mg/dose

Amoxicillin:clavulanate ratio = 50:10–50:17 = 5:1–3:1; out of the available formulations (2:1, 4:1, 7:1, 14:1, 16:1), 4:1 is the only one that is appropriate
